Crafting the Ultimate Flaky Pot Pie Crust

By Master Pastry Chef • 8 Min Read

A great chicken pot pie requires a top crust that shatter-shatters under the spoon, releasing aromatic chicken velvet steam. **Cold European Butter Lamination** cuts pea-sized butter pockets into unbleached pastry flour. When baked at high oven heat, water in the butter evaporates into steam pockets, creating dozens of crisp pastry layers. This guide explores pot pie crust baking.

1. Cold Butter Lamination Physics

Keeping high-fat European butter cold during dough mixing prevents fat melting before baking, ensuring distinct, crisp pastry layers.

2. Golden Egg Wash Glaze

Brushing pastry lids with a whisked egg yolk and cream glaze produces a golden mahogany sheen during wood-fired oven baking.
